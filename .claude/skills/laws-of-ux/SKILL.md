## Core Principles

- **Aesthetic-Usability Effect**  
  Make it look good → users trust it more and tolerate minor issues.

- **Choice Overload (Paradox of Choice)**  
  Limit options → fewer, clearer choices improve decisions.

- **Chunking**  
  Group information → break content into meaningful blocks.

- **Cognitive Bias**  
  Design with human irrationality in mind → guide decisions ethically.

- **Cognitive Load**  
  Reduce mental effort → simplify UI, minimize thinking.

- **Doherty Threshold (<400ms)**  
  Keep interactions fast → avoid perceived lag.

- **Fitts’s Law**  
  Bigger + closer targets → faster interaction.

- **Flow**  
  Remove friction → enable deep, uninterrupted focus.

- **Goal-Gradient Effect**  
  Show progress → motivate users near completion.

- **Hick’s Law**  
  Fewer choices → faster decisions.

- **Jakob’s Law**  
  Follow conventions → match user expectations from other products.

---

## Gestalt & Perception

- **Law of Common Region**  
  Group with boundaries → define sections visually.

- **Law of Proximity**  
  Place related items close together.

- **Law of Prägnanz**  
  Keep designs simple → users interpret simplest form.

- **Law of Similarity**  
  Use consistent styles → signal related elements.

- **Law of Uniform Connectedness**  
  Visually connect related items (lines, backgrounds).

---

## Mental Models & Behavior

- **Mental Model**  
  Align with user expectations → don’t fight intuition.

- **Miller’s Law (7±2)**  
  Limit visible items → avoid overload.

- **Occam’s Razor**  
  Choose simplest solution that works.

- **Paradox of the Active User**  
  Users don’t read → design for immediate use.

- **Pareto Principle (80/20)**  
  Focus on high-impact features.

- **Parkinson’s Law**  
  Constrain time/tasks → encourage efficiency.

---

## Experience & Memory

- **Peak-End Rule**  
  Optimize key moments and endings.

- **Postel’s Law**  
  Be forgiving in input, strict in output.

- **Selective Attention**  
  Highlight what matters → reduce distractions.

- **Serial Position Effect**  
  Put key items first or last.

- **Tesler’s Law**  
  Complexity cannot be removed → move it behind the scenes.

- **Von Restorff Effect**  
  Make important items stand out.

- **Working Memory**  
  Don’t overload → support with cues and context.

- **Zeigarnik Effect**  
  Show incomplete tasks → encourage completion.

---

## Quick Heuristics

- Reduce → simplify choices and UI  
- Group → structure content clearly  
- Highlight → guide attention intentionally  
- Align → match user expectations  
- Accelerate → keep interactions fast  
- Motivate → show progress and completion
